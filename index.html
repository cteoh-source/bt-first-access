The portal problem

Portals are built for buyers. Not for you.

Third-party listing portals provide useful exposure — but they also display information that can work directly against a vendor's best interest. First Access gives you the ability to control when and how that exposure begins.

1

Days-on-market works against you

Every day your home sits on a portal without an offer, buyers wonder what's wrong with it. First Access keeps days-on-market off the clock during Stages 0 and 1.

2

Automated valuations can misprice your home

Portal algorithms use incomplete data and outdated comparisons. Their published estimates can anchor buyer expectations below your actual market value before a single viewing.

3

Price drop history follows your listing

Any price reduction is permanently visible on portals — signalling desperation to every new buyer who finds your home. First Access helps you avoid this by validating price first.

4

Portal costs before a single buyer

A full portal campaign in Auckland can cost $3,000–$5,000 — all paid before you know if the right buyer even exists. If they're in B&T's database, you may never need to spend it.

Price protection

Every protection you'd have on the open market — at every stage.

The most common question vendors ask is whether a Stage 1 sale could mean a lower price. The answer is no — because every protection that exists in an open-market sale exists in Stage 1.

⚖️

Multi-offer protection is mandatory

If an offer arrives at Stage 1, every other registered buyer who has seen your property must be given the opportunity to submit their own offer. The same competitive dynamic that drives price at auction is built into Stage 1.

🎛️

You control the stage — always

You are never locked into accepting a Stage 1 offer. If matched-buyer response is thin or price feedback is below your expectation, you instruct your salesperson to move to Stage 2. No penalty. No days-on-market loss from Stage 1.

📋

You see the full picture before deciding

Before accepting any offer, your salesperson presents the complete matched-buyer report: how many buyers were alerted, how many expressed interest, how many declined. You make an informed decision — not a rushed one.

📄

Identical legal framework

A Stage 1 sale uses identical legal documentation to any open-market sale. Contract, conditions, settlement process — all the same. Your solicitor reviews the same documents. Nothing is bypassed or shortcut.
